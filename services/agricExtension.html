Agriculture Extension and Advisory

BACKGROUND

  1. The clamor for developing rural areas and providing a cushion to improve the total well-being of the communities has encouraged the need for agricultural development.
  2. It is expected that extension programmes will help increase farm productivity, farm revenue, reduce poverty and minimize food insecurity
  3. Extension services are primarily aimed at improving the knowledge of farmers for rural development; as such, it has been recognized as a critical component for technology transfer.
  4. ~Agricultural extension therefore is a major component to facilitate development since it plays a starring role in agricultural and rural development efforts

Our Goal

  1. To break down the barriers that block male and female farmers from accessing knowledge - a driving factor for improved production and revenue for families.The knowledge of various aspects of rural incomes will enhance the livelihoods of rural farmers.
  2. To increase farmer's productivity and income

What we do

  1. Incorporate the four main elements that can be identified within the process of agricultural extension: knowledge and skills, technical advice and information, farmers' organization, motivation and self-confidence.
  2. Through logical research and gathering of new information and initiatives we offer Agricultural extension services, incorporating a more extensive way of training rural people about agriculture, controls and agricultural marketing
  3. Provide advice, information, and other support services to farmers to enable them to improve the productivity of their crop and animal production and thereby their farm and non-farm incomes

Delivery

  1. The extension program delivery methodologies are constantly adjusted to meet farmers needs in their particular environmental and socio-economic settings.
  2. The particular approach used to deliver extension services in rural sectors will depend on the target farmers socio-economic and agro-ecological set-up.

Individual farm visits

  1. The main purpose of an individual farm visit is to identify and analyze the main problems affecting an individual farmer and to provide advice on the best actions

OUR PROJECT/ACTIVITIES

In partnership with Imara Foundation we are implementing a Gender Mainstreaming project for Alluvial Agricultural and Mastercard Foundation for an intervention project targeted at supporting 65000 smallholder farmers in 8 focal states in Northern Nigeria.

Our objectives are to :

  • Carefully source for women from the grassroots, residing in local areas from various communities in the Northern part of Nigeria to be 'Gender Champions'.
  • Organise trainings for the Gender Champions on:
    • Financial literacy/Financial inclusion
    • Various methods of Digital Data collection
    • Monitoring and Evaluation(M&E) tools: Baseline and follow up survey tools designed with a focus on enhancing Alluvial's recovery efforts.
    • Group dynamics
    • Basic business skills
    • Strategic skills/conflict resolution techniques
    • Data collection and reporting, digital data collection for use in field work, stepdown training techniques+roleplay, bookkeeping and farm records management
  • Participants will acquire a basic level of knowledge and competence in operating digital tools.
  • Digital access helps women refine their vocational skills (by taking online classes in agriculture, textile design, beautician training, and more), improve financial literacy, keep up with government programs that benefit their families.

The roles of the Gender Champions:

  1. Identify gender issues or unique challenges faced by women in communities(understand the social cultural barriers that women face).
  2. Bring out issues around the value chain, their challenges
  3. Provided stepdown training to the women lead farmers on financial literacy and basic business skills.
  4. Ensure that the records of women trained are kept- contact, locations, type of crop produced.
  5. liaise with sponsors on further areas to strengthen

The Gender Champions successfully:

  1. Conducted Stepdown training on Financial literacy and basic business skills in their various states for the women lead farmers
  2. Conducted filed work, proper data collection, used kobo tool and mobile devices to collect and store data.
  3. Carried out site visitation and engaged with the beneficiaries through effective communication
