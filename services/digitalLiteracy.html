Digital Literacy

This is the need for the acquisition of basic digital literacy skills for rural clusters in Nigeria.

  1. The benefits of the ability to use information and communication technologies to find, evaluate, create, and communicate information cannot be overemphasized, this requires both cognitive and technical skills.
  2. Digital literacy correlates with digital inclusion where people are able to use and access information and communication technologies, be able to do administrative procedures online via e-government services, make payments and financial services using technology, etc
  3. There are lots of opportunities that comes with being able to understand and use technology, having the skills you need to live, learn, and work in a society where communication and access to information is increasingly through digital technologies like internet platforms, social media, and mobile devices.
  4. Digital technologies play an important role in providing alternatives to the current problems and challenges facing rural territories in terms of production, marketing and development.

Why action is needed

  1. Nigerians have to adapt themselves to digital life in order to adapt to this digital world. There is a push to break the digital divide and increase digital literacy in the population for capacity building.
  2. Being excluded from information and communication opportunities is another form of poverty. The cost of digital means and mobile services are among the biggest barriers to digital literacy.
  3. We live in an information era, where internet access is the foundational tool used to access key information and digital media necessary to navigate our daily lives.

How we come in

  1. Aid in the understanding of how to use the internet and other technologies that is critical in helping the underserved achieve their goals. The opportunities that come with digital literacy can change the narratives for the women and youth in previously disadvantaged communities.
  2. We promote and execute digital skills training because developing those skills will allow for a qualitative leap in the lives of people living in rural areas.
  3. Participants will acquire a basic level of knowledge and competence in operating digital tools.
  4. Digital access helps women refine their vocational skills (by taking online classes in agriculture, textile design, beautician training, and more), improve financial literacy, keep up with government programs that benefit their families.

Monitoring and Impact assessment

We also monitor and assess the impact of the program. Once trained, the beneficiaries will be able to

  1. navigate the digital world send emails,
  2. connect on social media,
  3. use e-commerce websites,
  4. pay bills online,
  5. transact through digital payment modes,
  6. use maps and
  7. will also learn how to use the Internet to avail various services.

Outcome

  1. Strong digital literacy programme will encourage rural indwellers to acquire more skills, which will eventually lead to an increase in opportunities.
  2. We take advantage of smartphone penetration in rural areas to open their eyes to a myriad of technological development and opportunities.
  3. Digital literacy could potentially address some of the problems faced by small scale farmers in rural communities by equipping them with better access to agricultural experts, extension services and even the government.
